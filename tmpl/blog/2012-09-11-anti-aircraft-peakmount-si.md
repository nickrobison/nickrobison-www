As a newly transplanted flatlander, I&#8217;ve decided it&#8217;s high time I get some mountains under my feet. So while the weather holds I&#8217;m trying to do a trail a week at the various parks and mountains around Seattle/King County. So far, I&#8217;m holding strong.

Last week I made my way over to Bellevue to spend the day at <a title="Wikipedia Knows All" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cougar_Mountain_Regional_Wildland_Park" target="_blank">Cougar Mountain State Park</a>. Fortunately/Unfortunately I saw no cougars, feline or other. My goal was to hike to Anti-Aircraft Peak and take some sweet pictures, as I tend to do, I missed the sign for the peak and ended up settling for wandering around the various trails and paths until at long last I made my way completely around the park and up to the peak, which provides spectacularly obstructed views. Turns out, cougar mountain isn&#8217;t much of a mountain, think of a more rugged Pokagon, that&#8217;s about it. Still, it was an absolutely beautiful day and I managed about 8 miles through the trails in the late afternoon. As the views were mostly trees, I didn&#8217;t get very many good pictures, but here are a few:

Today (yesterday by the time you read this) I hit my first mountain, Mount Si [sigh], one of the most popular spots in the state, it&#8217;s usually packed with people but since it&#8217;s a Monday, and I went pretty late I passed only a few people going up and down. And really, it&#8217;s a lot of going up. The trail is pretty straight forward, 8 miles, 4 up, 4 down. Over its 4 miles it climbs over 3000 feet and let me tell you, I discovered that I am far more out of shape then I&#8217;d probably like to admit. Nevertheless, I made it to the top for some spectacular views of the Snoqualmie [snow-qual-me] Valley and even, a small glimpse of Seattle herself. The proper &#8216;summit&#8217; of this trail is a rock formation called The Haystack, which is scaled by the braver and more adventurous souls, of which I do not count myself a member, mostly because I was already tired and I was the only person at the summit, and in-spite of all my adventures, my coordination has not improved in the least, not a recipe for succes. Still, I enjoyed a nice rest on a substitute rock formation I scrambled up and read the scriptures whilst surveying the glory of God&#8217;s creation.
